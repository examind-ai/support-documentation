# Working with Online Proctoring

Our perspective on online proctoring has evolved. Before modern AI, much of the academic conversation — and our own emphasis — focused on assessment **design** to reduce the motivation and reward for cheating, and was skeptical of proctoring's overhead and false positives.

**That calculus changed with generative AI.** When any student can instantly generate high-quality answers, controlling exam conditions becomes essential for high-stakes remote assessment. We now see online proctoring as a **necessary complement** to good assessment design — not an alternative to it. For remote exams, proctoring has become part of the baseline for trustworthy results.

## How EXAMIND complements proctoring

The two approaches solve different halves of the problem:

* **Assessment design reduces the *payoff* of cheating.** Dynamic, per-student question variants and applied tasks mean a leaked or AI-generated answer is far less useful in the first place.
* **Proctoring controls the *opportunity* to cheat.** It verifies identity and deters unauthorized resources during the attempt.

Used together, they cover both the incentive and the opportunity — a stronger position than either alone.

## Using proctoring with EXAMIND

EXAMIND is **compatible with** leading online proctoring tools. This isn't a deep, single-launch integration — it works because of how these tools operate: the proctoring service supervises the browser session, and students launch the EXAMIND assessment through it (a third-party LTI launch).
